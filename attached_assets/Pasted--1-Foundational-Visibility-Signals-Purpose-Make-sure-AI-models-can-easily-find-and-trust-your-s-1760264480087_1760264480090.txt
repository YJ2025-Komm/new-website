🔹 1. Foundational Visibility Signals

Purpose: Make sure AI models can easily find and trust your site.

✅ Robots.txt and sitemap.xml present and accessible

✅ Canonical tags defined on all major pages

✅ Consistent URL structures (no parameter duplication)

✅ Correct hreflang tags for multilingual sites

✅ HTTPS enabled and valid SSL certificate

✅ Clear and crawlable navigation (no JS-only menus)

✅ Fast load speed and responsive design

✅ Indexable metadata (titles, meta descriptions, alt tags)

🔹 2. Authoritative Core (Facts AI Can Quote)

Purpose: Ensure your own site is the primary source for factual data about your brand/product.

✅ Pricing clearly stated in a structured, factual way (not hidden in images or popups)

✅ Features and capabilities described in precise, extractable sentences

✅ Integration lists with names, links, and categories

✅ About / Company / Team pages present with consistent data

✅ Security, compliance, and SLA statements explicitly mentioned (SOC 2, GDPR, ISO 27001, etc.)

✅ Changelog or release notes to show product evolution

✅ Support and contact pages with valid contact info

✅ Clear “last updated” markers on factual pages

✅ One canonical version of all factual statements (no conflicting pricing or claims)

🔹 3. Structured Data (Schema)

Purpose: Let AI models understand your content programmatically.

✅ Organization schema with name, logo, URL, contact info

✅ Website schema with search action

✅ SoftwareApplication or Product schema for core offering

✅ FAQPage schema for buyer questions

✅ HowTo schema for implementation guides

✅ Article or BlogPosting schema for blogs

✅ Review / AggregateRating schema if applicable

✅ BreadcrumbList schema for navigation clarity

✅ VideoObject schema (for video content)

✅ Consistent JSON-LD format across pages (no microdata/mixed types)

✅ No conflicting or invalid schema properties

🔹 4. Content Extractability

Purpose: Ensure AI systems can parse, summarize, and quote content accurately.

✅ Use of tables or definition lists for specs, pricing, limits, etc.

✅ Bullet points and short factual sentences rather than vague marketing copy

✅ Avoid text embedded inside images, SVGs, or videos

✅ Clear section headings (H2/H3) that answer buyer questions

✅ One paragraph summaries or “key takeaways” per article

✅ Consistent formatting for feature and integration descriptions

✅ Use of numerical data and measurable claims where possible

✅ Structured Q&A sections to answer common user intents

🔹 5. Coverage and Content Completeness

Purpose: Check whether all key buyer-intent pages exist and are discoverable.

✅ Homepage

✅ Pricing page

✅ Features overview

✅ Integrations list / marketplace

✅ Product documentation or API docs

✅ Security / Trust center

✅ FAQ

✅ Case studies or success stories

✅ Blog / resource center

✅ About / careers / contact

✅ Legal pages (Terms, Privacy, Cookie)

(Missing any of these is a visibility gap for AI models.)

🔹 6. Consistency & Contradiction Checks

Purpose: Detect where AIs might get confused by mismatched facts.

✅ Pricing consistent across schema, pricing page, blog mentions

✅ Feature names and limits consistent across pages

✅ Integration names consistent in schema, UI, and documentation

✅ Company name, tagline, and URLs consistent across metadata and copy

✅ Old or deprecated features not still indexed

🔹 7. Blog & Thought Leadership Signals

Purpose: Help AIs see your brand as an active, trusted expert source.

✅ Blog posts include author names and publication dates

✅ Consistent posting cadence (recent updates within last 60 days)

✅ Each post has clear topic summary and data sources cited

✅ Interlinking between blog posts and product pages

✅ Use of original insights or stats (helps EEAT)

✅ Rich snippets (FAQ / Article schema) for top blogs

🔹 8. External & Citation Signals

Purpose: Reinforce trust through third-party verification and mentions.

✅ Consistent company profile on Wikipedia, Crunchbase, G2, Capterra, etc.

✅ Analyst mentions (Gartner, Forrester, etc.) or reviews linking to your domain

✅ Social profiles linked in Organization schema

✅ Backlinks from reputable industry domains

✅ Press coverage or event participation pages linked from site

🔹 9. EEAT (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

Purpose: Reinforce credibility to AIs (and humans).

✅ Author bios for key content pieces

✅ Verifiable company address and contact methods

✅ Transparent policies and terms

✅ Cited data sources and references in educational content

✅ Testimonials or reviews with identifiable clients

✅ Secure and consistent brand identity across all touchpoints
